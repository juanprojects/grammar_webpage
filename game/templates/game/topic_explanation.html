Topic explanation

What is a describer?

The describer is the part of the Pre Modification of the Nominal Group that deals with descriptions of the Noun. In other words, we can say that inside the describer we find the characteristics of one noun.

Mainly, they are divided into two types: the opinion describer and the fact describer. Opinion describers are descriptions based on the personal perspective of the speaker/writer, whereas fact describers consider the characteristics that are facts. We can find the color, size, shape, among others, among the fact describers.

Describer ordering

Tale from a mundane writer:

"I remember once I got fuzzed at writing one of my novels. Once finished, I just had to put the title, but I struggled for three days thinking about which order was right: Was it The Dark Old Spanish Book right or The Old Spanish Dark Book? Or what about The Spanish Dark Old Book? What a trouble!

However, I realized there might be people with the same issue; I was right. Then I found Kayode's work (2022), and I finally could finish my novel (which was not as good as I thought it would be)."

According to Kayode (2022), epithets (or describers) are commonly placed as it follows:

Quality - Size - Shape - Age - Color - Material - Origin/Nationality - Participle

But we adapted it a little bit:

Opinion - Size - Shape - Age - Color - Material - Origin - Participle

We changed Quality for Opinion. At the end, they refer to the same notion, the subjectivity of the writer/speaker about the noun.

We also joined Origin and Nationality, as they meant the same concept: the place where someone was born or something comes from.

Types of describers:

We will proceed to explain describers (or epithets) by mentioning them in the order presented above.

Opinion: These are epithets that describe someone's own perception of the subject. This is to say, opinion epithets are the opposite of fact epithet (color, size, age, etc.).

Size: These epithets refer to the actual size of the noun. We can see adjectives as: little, big, small, huge, etc.

Shape: Generally, they deal with geometrical concepts such as: squared, rounded, crescent, etc.

Color: You might say that something is white, black, red, or green according to the real color of that thing. The color that we all perceive.

Age: Age Epithets handle the longevity that a thing or being has exited or lived.

Material/Origin: When saying what something is made of, you'd better use a material Epithet. For example, a wooden door, a plastic bag or a metal key. When, besides, saying the origin of something or someone, you'd better use origin epithets to say where someone was born or where something comes from.

Participle: These come from verb forms. They might appear in -ing form or -ed/en form. The -ing form refers to nouns that cause that verb to others, as in the boring teacher. On the other hand, the -ed/en form shows that the verb is affected at the noun, as in the bored teacher. (Lock, G (1996), pp. 49)

References:

- Lock, G (1996). Functional English Grammar An Introduction for Second Language Teachers. Cambridge University Press.

- Kayode A. (2022). AN APPRAISAL OF THE EPITHETS ORDERING IN THE MODIFIER SYSTEM OF NOMINAL GROUP. University of Ibadan.
