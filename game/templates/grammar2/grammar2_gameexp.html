Game Explanation

Generalities:

Hi, there! (again). Now I'm in charge of explaining the nature of the ga-game.

In general, you will practice the concepts before studied: simple and complex clause, parataxis and hypotaxis, macro and micro level of analysis; while using real data extracted from english literature.

Classification

That's right! in the first part, you will be given one excerpt from a book. You will have to tell if we're dealing with simple or complex clause.

If you choose the wrong option, it is completely fine. You'll see a message in which you'll be given a little help.

If you choose the right option in the first try, you will be rewarded with 2 points.

Boundaries

Now, for the second part, you will have to put the correct boundaries of the given excerpt using "//" for simple clause y "///" for complex clause.

If you choose the wrong option, it is completely fine. You'll see a message in which you'll be given a little help.

If you choose the right option in the first try, you will be rewarded with 4 points.

Labelling

Finally, in the third part you will have to label the clauses of the given excerpt according to their logical relationship; that is, parataxis or hypotaxis.

Remember that if we're dealing with parataxis, we label the independent clauses "1" and "2" respectively.

And if we're dealing with hypotaxis, we label the independent clause "α" (alpha), the dependent ones "β" (beta) and so on.

If you choose the wrong option, it is completely fine. You'll see a message in which you'll be given a little help.

If you choose the right option in the first try, you will be rewarded with 2 points.

Progression

At the end of each exercise you will be assigned the points you won. There will be 10 exercises.

And after completing the exercises, you will see your final score as well as the ones of the other people who have played the game.
