Simple and complex clause

Parataxis

Didn‚Äôt I tell you that illegal taxi races please me? He‚Ä¶he. It 's funny since those races also remind me of my Grammar II classes. Let me tell you why.

In the ‚Äúpiques‚Äù, we can see two taxis racing side by side. Neither depends on the other to successfully compete. Both taxis are at the same level as both are independent.

Now, let's imagine these two taxis are simple clauses. These two independent clauses are said to be in parataxis

To be in parataxis means that two clauses united by a conjunction can stand on their on. You can eliminate any of them and they will keep a complete meaning.

The conjunctions found in this kind of relationship are the FANBOYS: for, and, nor, but, or, yet and so.

We label the clauses as 1 for the first independent one and 2 for the second independent one

Hypotaxis

But don't forget about me!

My taxi is very likely to break down in the middle of races, so sometimes I need help from my bud Parakeet to tow me out of trouble.

When this happens, you can see that my taxi depends on Parakeet's taxi. The two taxis are not in the same level as mine is dependent while parakeet's is independent.

Now, let's imagine these two taxis are simple clauses. This dependent clause and this independent clause are said to be in hypotaxis.

To be in hypotaxis means that two clauses united by a conjunction have a dependent relationship. One clause cannont stand on its own because it needs the other one to have a complete meaning.

The conjunctions found in this kind of relationship are because, although, since, after, and many others. In short, the ones that are not part of the FANBOYS

We label the clauses as Œ±(alpha) for the independent one and Œ≤(beta) for the dependent one. If there is dependency within dependency, we label it Œ≥gama

Macro and micro level of analysis

Well, sometimes you will find that there are different levels of dependency in a complex clause. In these cases we need to analyse it in two levels: the macro level and the micro level

Analysis in the macro level means the main relationship among clauses, the one that conveys the full meaning of the sentence

Analysis in the micro level means the relationships within clauses, the ones that offers more information for the meaning of the sentence

Now we're going to explain to you about this phenomenon:

Hypotaxis within parataxis

Sometimes I think Parakeet is going crazy because, even in the middle of one of those illegal races, he's willing to pull my taxi along the run.

I usually relate this situation to hypotaxis within parataxis.

In this case, we would say that there is parataxis in the macro level and hypotaxis in the micro level.

Parataxis within hypotaxis

Crazy? I'm not crazy, I'm fearless! I'm so courageous that I can even tow two taxis and still win any race.

In this case, we would say that there is hypotaxis in the micro level and parataxis in the macro level
